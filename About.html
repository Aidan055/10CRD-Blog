My Big Idea is one of the last projects we're doing in Year 10 Creative Design. The general idea of the project is to to think and create an idea to change the wider community for the better.

In the project there were 8 foundations that we can choose for that contains topics that we can learnt about to create our impact and start our project.

These were:
Humanitarianism
Nutrition
Education
Healthcare
Elderly
Environment
Child Welfare
Animal Welfare

From there, the students will research these topics and find out the answer to some questions. These questions ranged from:

- What does this area of the community involve? (who, what, where etc)
- What are some of the issues in this area of the community? Find 5 examples
- What are some ways that people have tried to resolve these issues? Find 2 examples

After thinking of an idea, the students will finally take action. This is whether they will create a product, raise awareness, do a fundraiser, etc.

Other questions the students had to ask themselves through the project was:

- Is There A Need?

- What is the evidence that backs up the need for your idea?
- How might you find this information?

Another large part of the project was that everyone created a survey for people to answer in class, similar to what we do in the subject Community Action in the Innovation Stream, we had experience in how to do this. This is why in the end, each of us were able to have some statistical data to back our ideas, needs, and general questions to how we can create or change our ideas.

In addition to how we can do our project, we had to answer several questions as well.

- What will you do ?
- Who will you trial it on?
- Who/how will you ask?
- Where will you go?

From there, using Google Slides or other softwares, we had to document our process in the project we were doing. We then talked about the outcome of our project, whether it ended well or not, and then we had to present this to the whole class.
